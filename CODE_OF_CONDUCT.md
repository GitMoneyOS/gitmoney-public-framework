# GitMoneyOS Community Code Of Conduct

## Our Standard

GitMoneyOS exists to make AI-assisted work more owned, reviewable, and useful. Participation should make that work safer to inspect and easier to learn from.

Expected behavior includes:

- communicating with respect and practical clarity
- criticizing ideas, evidence, and systems without attacking people
- respecting privacy, consent, authorship, and attribution
- keeping client-owned, private, restricted, and secret information out of public contributions
- accepting review and correction without retaliation
- making space for nontechnical founders and nontraditional contributors

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, doxxing, credential exposure, deliberate privacy violations, impersonation, plagiarism, or publishing another person's work or story without permission.

## Scope

This standard applies in repository issues, pull requests, Discussions, events, and other spaces represented as GitMoneyOS community surfaces.

## Reporting

Do not post sensitive incident details publicly. The approved reporting alias is `conduct@hitsuyoaku.tech`. Do not send incident details until [PRIVATE-CHANNELS.md](PRIVATE-CHANNELS.md) records a passing delivery test; the owners must activate that lane before opening moderated community surfaces. Once active, include only the community surface, approximate date, behavior requiring review, immediate safety concern, and a reply route. Do not send unrelated private history, credentials, or client material.

## Enforcement

Repository owners may edit or remove contributions and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently remove participants when behavior threatens safety, privacy, trust, or useful collaboration. Decisions should be documented privately, use the least public detail necessary, and preserve evidence without redistributing harmful content.

## Owner And Review

Primary owner: Red Pillar (`redpillar75`).
Backup owner: Hitsuyo Aku (`HitsuyoAkuWeb3`). If a report concerns the primary owner, the backup owns triage. If it concerns both owners, no substantive enforcement decision is made until an independent reviewer is approved for that report.
Access: the primary and backup owner only, plus the approved independent reviewer when the conflict rule applies.
Response cadence: acknowledgment within two business days and a next-step update within five business days; these are operating targets, not guaranteed resolution times.
Minimum-data rule: preserve only the evidence needed to understand the reported behavior, protect participants, and document a proportionate decision.
Review cadence: annually and after any material enforcement event.
Approval path: changes require owner review through a pull request.
