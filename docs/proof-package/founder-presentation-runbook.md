# Founder Presentation Runbook

## Purpose

Guide a founder through GitMoneyOS in five minutes without overwhelming them with GitHub mechanics.

## Audience

Founders, operators, creators, agency owners, and small teams using AI before they have a clear operating record.

## Desired Feeling

"I can finally see how AI work becomes owned, approved, and easier to hand off."

## 90-Second Opening

Say:

> GitMoneyOS is plain-English GitHub for founders using AI. It turns scattered prompts, SOPs, decisions, docs, and workflows into an AI Office: a governed place where you can see what changed, who approved it, where knowledge lives, and what still creates risk.

Then say:

> We tested the concept on ourselves first. This public package shows the idea, the work trail, the quality bar, the self-audit, and the gaps we still need to close.

## Five-Minute Flow

### Minute 1: The Problem

Open: [Brand Narrative](brand-narrative.md)

Say:

AI makes work faster. But faster work without evidence creates risk.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can name where their own business knowledge is scattered.

### Minute 2: The Translation

Open: [Proof of Concept](proof-of-concept.md)

Say:

GitHub words become business controls.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can explain one translation, such as "a PR is an approval packet."

### Minute 3: The Work Trail

Open: [Proof of Work](proof-of-work.md)

Say:

This is not just a theory. Here are public issues, PRs, and repo structures that show the operating loop.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can see at least one public PR and one public issue.

### Minute 4: The Quality Bar

Open: [Proof of Quality](proof-of-quality.md)

Say:

The quality standard is evidence. GitMoneyOS found a governance gap in its own PRs and patched the system so that class of drift is easier to catch next time.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can explain why an incomplete PR body is a business risk.

### Minute 5: The Self-Audit

Open: [Self-Audit Summary](self-audit-summary.md)

Say:

Before auditing clients, GitMoneyOS audited itself. The result was strong enough to present, but honest about gaps.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can name the top three risks and next three actions.

## Close

Say:

> GitMoneyOS is not asking you to trust a pitch. It is showing the record: concept, work, quality, audit, and next actions.

## Call To Action

For a founder:

- Start with an AI Office Audit.
- Use the audit to map scattered knowledge, access risks, AI workflows, and GitHub readiness.
- Convert the audit into a Setup Sprint only when the evidence justifies it.

For a collaborator:

- Review the public proof package.
- Check the claim boundaries.
- Confirm what should stay public and what should stay private.

## Do Not Do

- Do not show private source doctrine.
- Do not open private repos unless the audience is approved.
- Do not claim production-grade readiness.
- Do not imply the SaaS dashboard is already shipped.
- Do not turn the demo into a GitHub tutorial before the founder understands the business reason.
