# Founder Presentation Runbook

## Purpose

Guide a founder through GitMoneyOS in five minutes without overwhelming them with GitHub mechanics.

## Audience

Founders, operators, creators, agency owners, and small teams using AI before they have a clear operating record.

## Desired Feeling

"I can finally see how AI work becomes owned, approved, and easier to hand off."

"This is what the future of serious small-team business infrastructure looks like."

## 90-Second Opening

Say:

> Most AI-powered businesses are creating output faster than they are creating control. GitMoneyOS is plain-English GitHub for founders using AI. It turns scattered prompts, SOPs, decisions, docs, and workflows into an AI Office: a governed place where you can see what changed, who approved it, where knowledge lives, and what still creates risk.

If the founder is new to the tools, add:

> Think of it as three rooms. Obsidian is the private notebook, AI tools are the work desk, and GitHub is the official company record. GitMoneyOS teaches what belongs in each room and how work moves between them.

Then say:

> The long-term thesis is simple: the companies that scale fastest will not just use AI. They will govern AI. Systems like this are how solo operators and small teams can pursue 9-figure scale with record-speed learning loops, without letting the business disappear into chat logs, contractor handoffs, and unapproved automation.

Then say:

> We tested the concept on ourselves first. This public package shows the idea, the work trail, the quality bar, the self-audit, and the gaps we still need to close.

## Sovereign 60

Use this when the room needs the sharpest possible opening.

**Indictment**

Most AI-powered businesses are producing faster than they can govern.

**Frame**

The winners will not be the teams with the most tools. The winners will be the teams with memory, agent boundaries, approval paths, and receipts.

**Proof**

GitMoneyOS tested that thesis on itself first: live GitHub organization, public/private repo map, owner structure, approval packets, public demo issue, proof package, self-audit, and remediation trail.

**Mechanism**

The mechanism is the Three-Layer Glass House: Source Memory, Agent Workbench, GitHub Record. Thinking becomes bounded work. Bounded work becomes evidence.

**Filter**

This is not for founders looking for another productivity hack. It is for operators who intend to scale serious business value without letting their knowledge, agents, and approvals scatter.

**Navigation**

Start with the proof package, then run the AI Office Audit.

## Five-Minute Flow

### Minute 1: The Problem

Open: [Brand Narrative](brand-narrative.md)

Say:

AI makes work faster. But faster work without evidence creates risk.

The hidden cost is not the AI bill. The hidden cost is operating knowledge that no one can inspect, approve, or hand off.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can name where their own business knowledge is scattered.

### Minute 2: The Translation

Open: [Three-Layer Glass House](three-layer-glass-house.md)

Say:

GitMoneyOS has three layers: Source Memory, Agent Workbench, and GitHub Record. The public proof package shows the workflow without exposing the private vault.

This is the leverage model: memory captures the doctrine, agents do bounded work, and GitHub turns approved work into receipts.

For a beginner, say:

The private notebook is for thinking. The work desk is for drafting. The company record is for approved truth.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can explain the three rooms without using technical language.

### Minute 3: The Work Trail

Open: [Proof of Work](proof-of-work.md)

Say:

This is not just a theory. Here are public issues, PRs, and repo structures that show the operating loop.

The point is not GitHub activity. The point is that the business can prove its own change history.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can see at least one public PR and one public issue.

### Minute 4: The Quality Bar

Open: [Proof of Quality](proof-of-quality.md)

Say:

The quality standard is evidence. GitMoneyOS found a governance gap in its own PRs and patched the system so that class of drift is easier to catch next time.

That is what serious systems do. They do not pretend the gap was never there. They turn the gap into a better rule.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can explain why an incomplete PR body is a business risk.

### Minute 5: The Self-Audit

Open: [Self-Audit Summary](self-audit-summary.md)

Say:

Before auditing clients, GitMoneyOS audited itself. The result was strong enough to present, but honest about gaps.

That honesty matters. A 9-figure standard is not hype. It is the discipline of knowing what the system can prove today and what evidence must exist before making the next claim.

Visible checkpoint:

- Founder can name the top three risks and next three actions.

## Close

Say:

> GitMoneyOS is not asking you to trust a pitch. It is showing the record: concept, work, quality, audit, and next actions. That is the future of founder infrastructure: fewer hidden decisions, fewer fragile handoffs, more proof.

## Call To Action

For a founder:

- Start with an AI Office Audit.
- Use the audit to map scattered knowledge, access risks, AI workflows, and GitHub readiness.
- Convert the audit into a Setup Sprint only when the evidence justifies it.

For a collaborator:

- Review the public proof package.
- Check the claim boundaries.
- Confirm what should stay public and what should stay private.

## Do Not Do

- Do not show private source doctrine.
- Do not open private repos unless the audience is approved.
- Do not claim production-grade readiness.
- Do not imply the SaaS dashboard is already shipped.
- Do not claim proven 9-figure outcomes.
- Do frame 9-figure scale as the operating standard this infrastructure is designed to support.
- Do not turn the demo into a GitHub tutorial before the founder understands the business reason.
