## Purpose

Help a founder see whether important business knowledge is easy to find, approve, protect, hand off, and improve.

Plain-English translation: this is not a valuation report. It is a visibility check. The score shows where the business record is clear and where important work still depends on memory, scattered tools, or unapproved AI output.

## What This Creates

The completed worksheet can become the founder's first official asset: a scored readiness snapshot with evidence, risks, and next actions.

It is useful because it turns vague concern into an inspectable record:

- what knowledge matters
- who owns it
- where it lives
- how it gets approved
- how it becomes reusable
- what still creates risk

## What This Is Not

This is not legal, financial, security, investment, or acquisition advice. A high score does not prove that a company is ready for diligence, a transaction, or a security review. It only shows that the business has stronger evidence, ownership, and handoff discipline than it had before.

## How To Score

Score each dimension from `0` to `5`.

Use observed evidence first. If you are guessing, write "inferred" and lower the score by at least one point.

| Score | Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| 0 | No reliable evidence exists. |
| 1 | The work exists mostly in a person's head, chat history, or scattered notes. |
| 2 | Drafts exist, but ownership, approval, or location is unclear. |
| 3 | The work is findable and repeatable, but not fully governed. |
| 4 | The work has an owner, approved location, and usable evidence. |
| 5 | The work is governed, versioned, reviewable, and has a maintenance cadence. |

## Score Bands

| Total | Plain-English Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| 0-10 | Hidden and fragile. Important knowledge is hard to inspect or hand off. |
| 11-20 | Scattered but recoverable. The business can see the gaps and start governing them. |
| 21-30 | Governable. The core assets are visible, but evidence and approval paths need strengthening. |
| 31-36 | Strong operating shell. The system is becoming handoff-readable, with specific gaps left to close. |
| 37-40 | Highly inspectable. The business record is unusually clear, but still needs ongoing review. |

## Data Boundary

Keep private or client-owned details out of this worksheet before sharing it publicly. Use public-safe examples, role names, and summarized evidence instead of client names, raw screenshots, credentials, access lists, private recordings, or confidential strategy.

## Done-When Checklist

- All eight dimensions have a score.
- Each score has observed evidence or is clearly marked inferred.
- The top three risks are named in plain English.
- The next three actions are small enough to start.
- The first official asset has an owner, approval path, evidence field, reuse path, and lifecycle.
- Any private, client-owned, restricted, or secret material has been removed or summarized safely.
