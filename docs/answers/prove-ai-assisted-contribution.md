# How Do You Prove An AI-Assisted Contribution?

## Short Answer

Show the problem, the contributor's judgment, the review, the accepted change, and the result. Raw activity counts are not enough.

## A Useful Proof Packet

1. **Problem:** What needed to change, and for whom?
2. **Role:** What did the person own or decide?
3. **AI boundary:** What did AI draft, retrieve, calculate, or propose?
4. **Judgment:** What was accepted, rejected, revised, or escalated?
5. **Evidence:** Which issue, pull request, design record, campaign asset, review, or release proves the contribution?
6. **Result:** What became clearer, safer, reusable, shipped, or measurable?
7. **Permission:** Who approved the public use of the story?

This works across code, design, documentation, research, marketing, operations, and project leadership. The evidence should make the contribution inspectable without pretending the person worked alone or that AI deserves ownership.

## Weak Signals

- number of prompts
- lines of code
- commit count without context
- generic AI-generated portfolio summaries
- private work described without permission
- claimed income or opportunity without a receipt

## Strong Signals

- a clear issue and accepted scope
- review comments showing judgment
- a before-and-after artifact
- a decision record explaining tradeoffs
- a release or outcome connected to the contribution
- a permissioned story with claim limits

## Evidence Boundary

Do not publish client evidence, private repositories, personal stories, income claims, or another contributor's identity without permission.
