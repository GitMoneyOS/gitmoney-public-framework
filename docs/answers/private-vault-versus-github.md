# What Belongs In A Private Vault Versus GitHub?

## Short Answer

Keep raw thinking, private identity, confidential evidence, and unfinished source material in the private vault. Put only approved business records and public-safe proof in GitHub.

## Private Vault

The private notebook can hold:

- unfinished doctrine and brain dumps
- private research and founder reflections
- confidential business strategy
- client-owned material under its proper access rules
- raw prompts, transcripts, notes, and source context
- ideas that have not passed an evidence or claim review

Private does not mean ungoverned. Sensitive notes still need ownership, classification, retrieval, backup, and lifecycle rules.

## Agent Workbench

The workbench receives the smallest approved context needed for a bounded job. It can create a draft, analysis, issue, decision packet, or proposed asset. The workbench is not the final authority.

## GitHub Record

GitHub should receive:

- approved operating procedures
- owner-reviewed agent rules
- decisions and change receipts
- public-safe frameworks and teaching material
- versioned product or delivery specifications
- contribution evidence that all named people have approved for use

## Promotion Test

Before moving anything out of the vault, ask:

1. Is the owner clear?
2. Is the classification clear?
3. Is this the minimum necessary information?
4. Has the claim or decision been reviewed?
5. Does the destination have the right visibility and access?
6. What evidence proves the promotion was approved?

The operating doctrine is simple:

> Do not push the vault. Push the approved record of the work.

## GitMoneyOS Route

Knowledge-business founders can use the [Exit-Ready Score](../exit-ready-score-self-assessment.md) and select the private-vault route in the [Public-Safe Audit Request](https://github.com/GitMoneyOS/gitmoney-public-framework/issues/new?template=audit-request.yml).

## Evidence Boundary

Never publish credentials, private client material, raw vault contents, private access maps, or restricted source doctrine to prove that a boundary exists.
