# What Makes AI Output An Official Business Asset?

## Short Answer

AI output becomes an official business asset when it has an owner, approval, evidence, a reuse path, and a lifecycle.

## The Five-Part Asset Test

| Control | Founder question | Evidence example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Owner | Who is accountable for this being correct and useful? | Named person or role |
| Approval | Who accepted it, and under what rule? | Pull-request review, signed checklist, decision record |
| Evidence | What proves the current version and the decision? | File, commit, issue, test, screenshot, or confirmation |
| Reuse path | What real job will this perform again? | Onboarding, delivery, sales, governance, training, handoff |
| Lifecycle | When is it reviewed, changed, archived, or retired? | Date, trigger, cadence, or owner review |

Without those controls, the output may still be a useful draft. It is not yet a governed asset.

## Example

An AI drafts a customer-onboarding playbook.

- The Delivery Lead owns it.
- The founder reviews and approves it through a pull request.
- The merged file and test walkthrough are the evidence.
- The team uses it for every new customer.
- It is reviewed after five onboardings or once per quarter.

The asset is not valuable because AI wrote it. It is valuable because the business can trust, reuse, inspect, and improve it.

## First Official Asset

Start small. Choose one decision, procedure, scorecard, prompt, agent rule, specification, or contribution that already matters. Run the five-part test and place the approved record in the correct private or public repository.

Use the [seven-day First Official Asset Challenge](../challenges/first-official-asset.md) for a guided path.

## Evidence Boundary

An official asset does not become public by default. Visibility follows data classification, owner permission, and the minimum necessary evidence rule.
