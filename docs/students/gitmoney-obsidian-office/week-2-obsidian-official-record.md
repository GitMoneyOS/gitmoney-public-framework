## Purpose

Week 1 gave students the official record: GitHub, the vault for approved business assets.

Week 2 answers the question every student hits next: **where does the work come from before it deserves the record?**

The answer is GitMoney Obsidian Office: a private memory layer where raw thought is captured, shaped, and matured until it earns promotion.

## The Three Layers

Say it until it is boring:

- **Obsidian is private memory.** Raw captures, half ideas, research clips, drafts, doctrine. Nobody sees it. Nothing in it is official.
- **Agent platforms are workbenches.** You bring a brief, the agent does a scoped job, the output is a draft with evidence.
- **GitHub is the official record.** Only approved work lands here. Every promotion has an owner, an approval path, and a receipt.

Most people fail by collapsing the layers: they treat their notes app as their business record, or they let an AI publish drafts as if they were decisions. The layers exist so excitement cannot skip approval.

## The Loop

```text
Capture in Obsidian -> Shape with AI Office skill -> Route through agent workbench -> Promote approved work to GitHub -> Review monthly
```

- **Capture**: one idea per note, into the inbox, with owner, source, status, classification, and summary.
- **Shape**: the skill helps you classify the note, link it, and score whether it is an asset candidate.
- **Route**: if work is needed, the note becomes an agent brief and moves across the Workbench Board.
- **Promote**: approved work becomes a GitHub issue, repo doc, decision log entry, content asset, or SOP.
- **Review**: weekly for the inbox and board, monthly for the vault's health.

## The Maturity Ladder

Every note carries a status:

`seed -> sprout -> asset-candidate -> ready-to-promote -> promoted` (or `archived` at any point).

A seed costs nothing. An asset candidate has to justify itself. Nothing reaches `promoted` without passing the asset test: owner, approval path, evidence, reuse path, lifecycle.

## Week 2 Assignment

1. Install the starter vault and complete the 30-minute win in `START_HERE.md`.
2. Capture five real ideas from your own business into the inbox.
3. Turn one of them into an agent brief and move it across the Workbench Board.
4. Identify one asset candidate from your own notes, prompts, workflows, content, or business knowledge.
5. Decide its promotion target: GitHub issue, repo doc, decision log, content asset, SOP, or none.
6. Post your proof in Discord; record the decision in your vault.

## Completion Proof

A student has completed Week 2 when they can:

- Explain the three layers without notes.
- Show one note with owner, source, status, classification, summary, and promotion target.
- Show one agent brief created from a note.
- Show one work item that moved through the Workbench Board.
- Name one asset candidate from their own material and say what stays private and what gets promoted.

No live GitHub access is required to get value from this kit. Promotion can be queued until the student's organization is ready.
