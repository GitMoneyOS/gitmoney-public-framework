## Purpose

Repo Man is your first builder vault.

> You do not just collect repos. You create a GitHub organization, fork useful tools, justify why each belongs in your business, capture risk and evidence, then turn the work into your first official business record.

The checklist is the path. The intake form is the gate. The proof packet is the receipt.

## Part 1: Repo Man Starter Checklist

Complete in order. Each step produces evidence.

1. **Create your GitHub organization.** Name it `[YourName] Repo Man`. This is your builder vault, not a code dump.
2. **Run the GitHub setup wizard.** Use the engineered setup wizard prompt (not the raw version) through your agent platform to configure security, admin controls, and user settings.
3. **Capture setup evidence.** Screenshot your live organization settings after the wizard finishes. This is your first receipt.
4. **Fork the approved starter repo.** Fork the Get Money Public Framework or the approved starter repo into your organization.
5. **Find three additional public repos** that are useful to your business. Public, licensed, legitimately accessible tools only.
6. **Complete a Repo Intake Form for each repo** (Part 2 below) before it earns a place in your vault. No justification, no fork.
7. **Clone your keepers locally.** Store them in a stable, organized location your agent can reach. Not Downloads.
8. **Open your first official business issue** in your organization: what you will build first with one of your repos, and what proof will show it worked.
9. **Post your proof in Discord, record it on GitHub.** Discord is where the room moves. GitHub is where the record lives. Any decision made in Discord becomes an issue, note, or decision log.
10. **Set your next review date.** Every repo in the vault gets re-reviewed: keep, test, archive, or reject.

Risk level guide:

- **Low**: reads public data, produces documents or content, touches nothing sensitive.
- **Medium**: needs API keys or accounts, automates communication, or publishes on your behalf.
- **High**: touches money, trading, client data, credentials, or permissions. High-risk repos require a human-reviewed experiment before any live use.

> A free tool is not free if it creates unmanaged risk.

## Safety Boundaries

- Use public open-source resources responsibly. Respect licenses, permissions, credentials, and platform rules. This lab never involves bypassing access controls or using anything you do not have a legitimate right to access.
- Push the approved student package and the record of your work. Do not push private skill internals, owner-only material, keys, or credentials.
- Instructor access for spot checks is inspection-only: lowest workable role, defined duration, no billing, secrets, destructive changes, or merges without approval, with removal at the offboarding checkpoint.
