Recorded: 2026-07-03

Layer classification: GitHub record

## Lesson Objective

Take one piece of raw founder data and move it through the full GitMoney AI Office until it becomes a trackable business asset with an owner, an approval path, evidence, and a feedback signal.

This is the synthesis class. It does not teach a new tool. It reveals what the four lessons were building the whole time.

## The Reveal

GitMoney was never about Obsidian. It was never about agents. It was never about GitHub.

It was about building a business that can learn from itself.

Most founders do not have a marketing problem. They have a memory problem. Their business keeps generating evidence — client calls, objections, content reactions, sales notes, testimonials, refunds, silences, failed launches, half-built assets, ideas trapped in voice notes. None of it becomes a closed loop. It just becomes clutter.

A business that cannot inspect itself cannot improve itself. A business that cannot improve itself cannot scale without the founder becoming the missing interface.

GitMoney closes the loop:

```text
Evidence -> Diagnosis -> Routing -> Execution -> Reporting -> Revenue Signal -> New Evidence
```

In plain GitMoney language:

```text
Obsidian captures the raw truth.
Agents turn the truth into reviewable work.
GitHub stores the approved record.
Revenue signals tell the system what was real.
The result becomes new source material.
```

## The Loop As The Master Operating Model

```text
1. Evidence
   Raw signal from the founder, market, clients, content, sales, operations.

2. Diagnosis
   What does the evidence reveal? A signal leak, offer gap, trust break,
   workflow failure, unclear asset, or missing proof.

3. Routing
   Where should this go? Content, sales, delivery, nurture, a GitHub issue,
   an SOP, a case file, an offer update, a rejection, or the archive.

4. Execution
   The founder, operator, or bounded agent creates the asset, task,
   checklist, report, page, or system update.

5. Reporting
   What happened? What changed? What shipped? What proof exists?
   What is still risky?

6. Revenue Signal
   What value was captured? A booked call, a qualified reply, a referral,
   a testimonial, saved time, reduced risk, a retained client, money.

7. New Evidence
   The result feeds the next note, next issue, next report, next offer
   refinement, next asset.
```

The GitMoney translation:

```text
Evidence lives in Obsidian.
Diagnosis happens through founder judgment plus bounded agents.
Routing becomes GitHub issues.
Execution happens through agent workbenches and operators.
Reporting becomes PRs, commits, dashboards, changelogs, and review notes.
Revenue signals become proof.
Proof becomes new source material.
```

## Worked Example: One Signal Through The Whole Loop

Example status: fictional, public-safe, non-client-specific.

The raw signal: a founder keeps hearing "your work is powerful, but I don't understand what you actually sell."

**1. Evidence** — an Obsidian note:

```text
Raw Evidence:
Audience understands the vibe but cannot repeat the offer.

Source:
DMs, sales calls, comments, low conversion despite high engagement.

Observed Pattern:
Attention is present. Buyer clarity is absent.
```

**2. Diagnosis** — name the actual problem:

```text
Diagnosis:
Signal leakage. The brand has meaning, but the offer interface is weak.

Business Risk:
The founder is forced to explain the value manually on every call.
```

**3. Routing** — create a GitHub issue:

```text
Title:
Fix homepage offer clarity leak

Issue Type:
Brand asset / conversion risk

Why It Matters:
The page is not carrying the founder's value without manual translation.

Done When:
- One-sentence offer is rewritten
- Homepage hero section updated
- Call to action clarified
- Before/after screenshot attached
- Founder approval recorded
```

**4. Execution** — a bounded agent brief:

```text
Agent Task:
Turn the raw evidence and diagnosis into three homepage hero options,
one final recommendation, and a rationale.

Human Review:
Founder chooses the final version. The agent does not publish anything.
```

**5. Reporting** — the pull request:

```text
What Changed:
Homepage hero message updated to reduce offer ambiguity.

Evidence Used:
DM pattern, call notes, website review.

Expected Outcome:
More qualified clicks and fewer "what do you do?" questions.

Review Needed:
Founder approval before merge.
```

**6. Revenue Signal** — define what feedback will grade the work:

```text
Revenue Signals To Track:
- Booked calls
- Better-fit leads
- Faster sales conversations
- Higher reply rate
- Reduced explanation burden on calls
```

**7. New Evidence** — the loop continues:

```text
New Evidence:
After the hero update, people understand the offer but hesitate on price.

Next Issue:
Build a proof section to reduce the trust gap.
```

That is one full metabolic cycle: a vague frustration became a diagnosed leak, a routed task, an approved asset, a measurable signal, and the seed of the next improvement — with receipts at every step.

## Capstone Assignment: Build Your First Cybernetic Revenue Loop

Pick one piece of raw founder data from your own business and run the complete loop. Submit:

```text
1. One Obsidian evidence note
2. One diagnosis summary
3. One GitHub issue
4. One routed execution task or agent brief
5. One generated or improved business asset
6. One report or PR-style summary
7. One revenue signal to track
8. One follow-up issue created from the result
```

The deliverable is not a pretty document. The deliverable is a business learning loop with receipts.

Follow the proof-package rule: show the workflow, protect the source. Your submission should demonstrate concept, work trail, and quality bar without exposing private client data, credentials, pricing, or anything you have not approved for sharing.

## Scoring Rubric (20 Points)

**1. Evidence quality — 4 points.** Did you use real business signal, or vague founder feelings?

```text
Weak:   "I got 12 likes."
Strong: "Three prospects asked the same objection before going silent."
```

**2. Diagnosis quality — 4 points.** Did you name the actual problem?

```text
Weak:   "Need better content."
Strong: "The offer creates attention but does not create buyer certainty."
```

**3. Routing quality — 4 points.** Does the issue route the problem to the right next action?

```text
Weak:   "Make brand better."
Strong: "Rewrite landing page hero to clarify buyer, pain, promise,
         proof, and next step."
```

**4. Execution quality — 4 points.** Did you produce a usable asset, SOP, checklist, report, page section, campaign, or sales artifact? No vapor. No "strategy coming soon." Ship something reviewable.

**5. Feedback quality — 4 points.** Did you define what signal will determine whether it worked?

```text
Good signals: booked calls, qualified replies, sales objections,
time saved, conversion rate, client approval, retention, referrals.
```
